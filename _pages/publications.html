My research lies at the intersection of environmental economics, innovation economics, and energy policy. I study how market structure, competition, and technological linkages shape the pace and direction of electrification and decarbonization. A central focus of my work is understanding how imperfect competition interacts with directed technical change — and what this means for the design of effective climate and innovation policy.
